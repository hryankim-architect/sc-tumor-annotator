# v0.1 — initial capability portrait

**Type:** new repository (capability portrait #7 in the portfolio)

## What this is

A clean-room POC of a *class* of capability for cancer single-cell RNA-seq:
trainable tree-based hierarchical cell-type annotation, expression-based
copy-number-variation (CNV) inference with a chromosome-length-normalized score,
and cancer subtype / grade prediction within malignant cells. Built entirely
from public methods (Scanpy, InferCNV, CopyKat, scikit-learn) on synthetic,
deterministically-generated data. No proprietary material is present.

## Shipped in v0.1

- `synth.py` — deterministic synthetic cancer scRNA-seq with chromosome-mapped
  genes, stromal + epithelial compartments, CNV-imprinted malignant cells, and
  ER+/HER2+/TNBC subtype + grade-1/2/3 signatures.
- `cnv.py` — genome-ordered, reference-centered, within-chromosome smoothing;
  plus a chromosome-length-normalized aggregate CNV score and a parameter-light
  malignancy call.
- `annotate.py` — four-stage `HierarchicalAnnotator` (gradient-boosted trees)
  with the CNV score as a malignant-call feature, and a kNN
  `ReferenceMappingBaseline` for head-to-head comparison.
- `evaluate.py` — 5-fold stratified CV and independent-cohort hold-out.
- `pipeline.py` — CLI entry wiring audit + MLflow hooks around the body.
- Substrate hooks (`audit.py`, `tracking.py`, `canary.py`), CI (ruff + pytest +
  scope-preamble lint + canary + English-only scan), MIT license, Makefile.

## Demo metrics (synthetic, seed 0; macro-F1)

| Axis | 5-fold CV (tree) | Independent (tree) | Independent (baseline) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cell type | 0.998 | 0.998 | 1.000 |
| Malignant call | 1.000 | 1.000 | 1.000 |
| Subtype | 1.000 | 1.000 | 1.000 |
| Grade | 0.938 | 0.910 | 0.874 |

Tree-based model beats the CNV-blind baseline on the hardest axis (grade) on
both CV and the independent cohort; other axes are saturated by design on the
separable synthetic data. CNV-score separation between malignant and normal
cells on the demo cohort ≈ 0.23 (canary requires ≥ 0.05).

## Honest scope

The numbers describe the synthetic demo only and are not a benchmark claim. See
`what-is-out-of-scope.md`.
